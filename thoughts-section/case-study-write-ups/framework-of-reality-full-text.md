## Framework of Reality - Ultimate Summary

## EXP pt. 1 - BENEFIT [WHY SHOULD I CARE ABOUT SPIRITUAL KNOWLEDGE/MEANING?]

- Unhappiness, or depression, comes from a lack of clarity, a lack of meaning; offeeling lost, and not seeing the point in anything
- Happiness is a byproduct of clarity, of knowing who you are and what you’re here to do i.e. feeling motivated/knowing what’s worth doing
- Hence the saying “Give a man a purpose and the ability to achieve it, he’ll walk over broken glass with a smile”
- Purpose can create Meaning in an easy life, while reducing the suffering of a difficult life
- Spirituality, metaphysics, philosophy and religion are there to serve this purpose, to help us understand our existence and our place within it
- To help you figure out who you are, why you exist, how you should be, and therefore what therefore you should do
- This is called our “frame of reality” which gives you a clarity of mind, and fulfilling our innate need for Existential clarity
- It is the fulfilment of this need that is intimately connected with our experience of Meaning and Purpose
- In summary, your “frame of reality” informs you on what’s important, or meaningful,for you to do
- This knowledge of meaning is a sustainable source of motivation, happiness and peace
- But the problem is that our world is too noisy; there’s too many beliefs and stories out there, so it’s hard to really commit to one frame of reality, and feel the benefits of that clarity

## EXP pt. 2 - MEANING [HOW DO I FIND THE MEANING OF LIFE?]

- How can we achieve this clarity and confidence in a world that bombards us with a vast ocean of new information, values, ideas, and perspectives?
- By knowing that, at the end of the day, religion/spirituality/philosophy; all frames of reality - even simulation / soul slave trap theory - are all stories
- If we reverse engineered what exactly makes a story Meaningful, we can identify the most important value that resonates with us on the deepest level
- Hence the saying “Know the way broadly and see it in all things”
- The universal essence of meaning in all stories is found in how it affected us; how we feel a Connection with it, and how we learn and Grow from it
- For example, the moral of a story was never about what happened to the character, but how they had changed, and who they had become
- The essence of meaning in stories also point to the patterns of our own meaningful experiences

We find this clarity by thinking about our own idea of meaning. Looking more closely, in every meaningful experience we either:

## I. learn about ourselves or the world,  II. experience joy/love or  III. create something of value

- Therefore the meaning of an experience can be measured by how significantly we learnt from it, how much wiser we are now able to love because of it, and how much now we are able to create as a result of it

At the root of these patterns, in every meaningful experience, we see a certain direction. Meaning is experienced when a certain progress was made in the scale of either:
I. personal Growth and/or
II. Connecting deeper with something larger than ourselves.

- Why is that true about life? Because if something is not Growing, it’s dying. Life Grows when it Connects, and vice versa
- The core of who we are, life itself, is Growing from within us, through us. Life itself, is Connecting with us, through us, all around us

From a purely intellectual perspective, we should devote ourselves to learning about, loving and Connecting with the source of life within us, knowing that everytime we do so, we Grow in the direction of our meaning; purpose, happiness and vice versa.

- In other words, to experience the clarity, happiness and purpose that comes from discovering the Meaning of life, you have to see that your meaning is life’s meaning
- And therefore, the more you want to understand the Meaning of life, the more you must learn about the essence of life, of existence itself

It’s also pertinent to note the “existentialism” that can often come with “spiritual” thinking. As many gurus and teachers espouse “non-dual” thinking regarding life, it becomes easy to conclude that nothing material has any meaning. It is argued that since this life is “just an illusion” then there’s no point in caring about anything. This is an easy metaphysical trap to fall into when deciding on our frame of reality.

A related trap to the “illusion” paradox is the narrative that we are simply living in a simulation. The belief is that this simulation is expertly designed to trick us into exerting effort into a “fake” existence, so that our energy can be utilised for other means. As such, everything up to and including this document of teachings can be viewed as being a part of this untruth. However, in the case of the rational in the Framework of Reality, we are interested in the lived experience of meaning, rather than thinking about meaning. It could also be said that this simulation is “programmed” to enable us to experience meaning under certain conditions. If this is true, then the knowledge of those conditions remains equally as important to understand.

Whatever mechanism of existence, whether it be an evil simulation or simply God’s divine dream, we still see the same underlying theme of the inevitable victory of good over evil. We see the moulding and forging of the being through its own merit, through the complete victory of faith over fear. There may always be a complete victory, of positivity over negativity, creativity over
destruction, and of virtue over vice. And it is achieved because it is potentially possible for ourselves.

## VALUES - EXISTENCE/LIFE/GOD [WHAT IS THE MEANING OF LIFE?]

- To Connect with the source of life within us, we must further zoom into the root of it; to understand what life actually is, and find that all else flows from there.
- The source of existence/God/life itself is infinite, because it created the mere concept of infinity; it formed the mere fabric of that which we measure space and time
- If we were to ask what the Meaning of this Existence/creation/life is, we’re actually asking, “what is the intention of an infinite and intelligent source of life/creation?”
- By definition, the most fundamental nature of life itself is to grow, for when life ceases to grow, it begins to die.
- Growth is the most fundamental nature, intention, and therefore Meaning, of life itself.

Considering the infinite and intelligent nature of the source of life/creation, it can be deduced that life’s Growth manifests through an endless process of:
I. Self-discovery - as part of an inherently intelligent source, life seeks to deepen its understanding of its own nature and creative abilities (an infinitely intelligent force would exist in a perpetual state of learning about its infinite self)
II. Joy/Love - as a positive, nurturing force, love contributes to the overall well-being and development of life itself, aligning with this conceptual intention of an infinite/perfect source of creation.
III. Creation - our first hand experience of Existence is evidence of how life expresses its infinite creative nature.

This deduction can be viewed as a personification of the infinite source of existence and existence itself. However, it may be rational to draw such an image, based on the assumption that the fundamental essence of this source is consciousness in nature. Additionally, if one were to further personify the infinite source of existence and existence itself, they could deduce two more traits or Values.

- “God is bored” - being everything, having experienced everything, there is nothing that God hasn’t done. As such, “he” may have created everything, including you, from a place of boredom.
- “God is lonely” - by definition, there is nothing, or nobody, outside of the infinite source of existence and existence itself. If this source were conscious, it may exist in a perpetual state of infinite loneliness. It may orient itself out of this state by “forgetting” and separating itself into individual, veiled consciousnesses in different living creatures - who remain free from this loneliness.
- In summary, the meaning of life itself is to Grow, and its Growth manifests through an understanding of itself and its ability to love, expressing these through creation
- Additionally, the meaning of life is to not take things seriously, to aim to have fun - forget about existentialism - and enjoy each other’s company (even if it’s all just a simulation)

We have an inner Purpose to Grow through:
I. self-discovery,
II. self-love and
III. self-transcendence.

We have an outer Purpose to Grow through:
I. learning about the world,
II. contributing to the world’s love and
III. creating a better world.

Our inner and outer Purpose/goals are intertwined, since we can use our goals to learn about ourselves through our Connection to ourselves, others and the world around us. This can be seen as the Middle Way of life; intentional, purposeful enjoyment, of finding meaning in the way we Grow through our worldly tests.

## STRATEGY - PRINCIPLES [HOW SHOULD I BE]

- As we become someone who makes choices in service of our Growth-oriented Purpose, our Principles can become a reflection of the decision making process of life itself
- Principles are what we use to ensure that our decisions are aligned with our Purpose, or Growth

If you want to experience meaning, then consider the following before making a decision:
I. Truth and integrity: seek the truth and act in alignment with your truth
II. Loving devotion: seek the wellbeing of yourself and those around you
III. Empowered responsibility: seek to fulfil your highest potential for Growth and Connection
IV. Wholeness and balance: seek balance in truth, love and power. Try to have fun, too
$V$. Presence: seek clarity of mind to align with your principles by overcoming negative emotions/distractions. Have the courage to recognise and accept negative feelings in your body, and make an effort to nurture them. Your decision making abilities are vastly improved when you are free of stress

Following this logic, to find the objective essence of Meaning within all stories is to find a foundational truth that exists across all of them.
