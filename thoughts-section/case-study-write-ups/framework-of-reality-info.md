# Framework of Reality Full Conversion Story

**Situation:** Even after writing my book, I still found it hard to summarise all my insights on life/metaphysics (religion, philosophy, spirituality).

**Challenge:** It was hard to organise and recall all this information in my mind.

**Desire:**  I wanted to connect this knowledge in a way I that could easily remember and feel clear about, so that I could articulate it in conversations.

**Decision:** I decided to hierarchically structure this knowledge and distil it down onto one page, by applying a metacognitive approach I use in product strategy. I also took note of this approach in my TEDx talk, highlighting the usefulness of strategies that businesses use but for our own existential clarity.

As metaphysics explores our purpose knowledge of ourselves, existence, and our place within it; businesses strategize through knowledge of their capabilities, their industry and their place in the market. This knowledge is organised in 5 categories of a businesses’: i) Backstory (Experiences) which informs their ii) Values (Who), then their iii) Vision/Purpose (Why), then their iv) Strategy (How), then which informs their v) Tactics (What).

I therefore decided to consolidate all metaphysical knowledge under those headings in the same order, to make a conclusive strategy for our own life.

**Breakthrough Journey:** This same structure aligned with most pertinent existential questions: what’s the meaning of life, who am I, why do I exist, how should I be, and then, so what; what should I actually do, right now, with this information? So chronologically, the subheadings went like this:

1. In terms of Backstory, we look back at when we experienced meaning. Meaning is experienced when we grow, characterised by a deeper connection transcendent capacity within the self, others, and world.
2. I asked why, which lead me to Values or “Who am I” and realised it’s because we are life; life’s meaning is our meaning, hence why we should value things that are synonymous with life.
3. Our vision or purpose, therefore, would be to grow in our identification and connection with, and expression of, this intention of life, as we experience meaning when we become one with this capacity within us.
4. Then our strategy, is to make decisions based on that which will help us achieve this. Hence why we should act from life’s values. This requires being present, so we can overcome distraction and make clearer aligned decision-making.
5. This informs the final subheading, Tactics, which is the “so What” of what we should actually do right now – which is to be present. This means recognizing, accepting, and nurturing internal feelings with equanimity, to gain clarity of mind and further connect us to the truer core of life that we are.

This felt like a satiating breakthrough, as I could see the link between the first and last subheading, meaning and presence; seeing that the conclusion of knowledge wasn’t an intellectual fact, but a state of being.

**Epiphany/Importance:** And the reason I shared that is because… the best way to feel clear about the answer on the meaning of life, is to know that the more you let go and get present… and relax… in a state of serene certainty, the more you’re already there. In other words, the best way to find the answers you’re looking for, is to align yourself with how you’d be if you’d just found your answer.

That’s why the Framework of Reality is the most effective remedy to constant thinking or searching for new information. It does this by logically taking you, step-by-step to your conclusive answer, showing you exactly how the meaning of it all is to grow your connection with this transcendent capacity within yourself.

This reminded me of the same esoteric conclusion from my book, which was reaffirming. My book similarly aimed to unify all metaphysical stories, by looking at them under the lens of esoteric symbolism. The meaning of Adam and Eve, for example, is about listening to, or aligning with, the will of God, the creator, or life, over our lower egoic temptations. When we stray from the will of God, or life itself, we lose our meaning, or our perfect garden of Eden.

This insight is also why the Framework of Reality is also the best way to find meaning even in more mundane tasks. It highlights how, since our outer goals help us grow or connect with ourselves, others or the world, then they also serve a meaningful purpose. This was particularly important realisation for me because I always struggled to integrate the meaning of my more material goals with my more existential or spiritual beliefs. And now I had a newfound motivation towards my everyday responsibilities or more “mundane” tasks.

Overall, summarising metaphysical knowledge with these 5 subheadings that feed into each other hierarchically, like a tree with branches, is the best way to organise and recall all the information in your mind.

**Importance:** It actually helps with conversation, not just because I can articulate this wisdom better but, because it gave me a new direction for my conversations. I could see that the meaning of conversation itself would be to share this benefit of meaning with others, by helping people relieve stress and reconnect back to themselves and to find that peace and contentment within themselves.

Our romantic relationships are the ultimate tests of this, by the way. We have to find this meaning within ourselves first, and not rely on our partner for that fulfilment. It’s hard because we get so attached, to their approval, but if we abide by our principles, or otherwise known as sticking to our conscience; doing what’s right, not looking to anyone to make us happy but ourselves, that’s how you have a love that lasts.

This is because you’re able to bring your best, most magnetic self to others when you’re following your own Conscience. When you’re clear on your values, like the one shown in this Framework of Reality, and abide by them, you feel better about yourself, and just about life in general. You have a much more self-reliant, attractive energy to be around because you’re more confident, present, and self-aware.

And you can do it too from the link in my bio; I’ve turned it into a story so there’s a story edition first to help you learn it, then a longer non-fiction version with just the learnings, and then a two-pager so you can remember it. I personally recommend printing out this page and keeping it with you, reading and reminding yourself of it every day, just as I do, to keep you on track and give you that feeling of finally finding your answers.
