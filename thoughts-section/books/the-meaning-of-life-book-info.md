# **Synopsis**

“When I’m reading a good book, it feels like I’m searching for something… I don’t know what I’m looking for, but I know that when I find it I’ll feel more at peace - more at one - with myself.” - Adam, The Proposition

Do you ever feel like you’ll never have enough time to read all the knowledge out there… about life, existence, and our role within it?

It’s difficult these days to decide between whether to be a religious person, follow even more ancient sages, adopt a purely rational approach to life, or make your own mix of everything… Sometimes you may even come across a satisfying conclusion, only for it to get swept away by the tide of new information, ideas and perspectives.

“The Meaning of Life” is a short spinoff story of from a fiction book “The Proposition” that brings together an entire library of new and ancient knowledge, this time in an even more raw, structured and distilled form.

This book has one aim: to satiating that deep yearning in your gut, that wants to grasp the answers to all of life’s most important questions. How? By knowing that there are only 5 questions that encapsulate the entire spectrum of metaphysical subjects; religion, philosophy and spirituality:

- Where did I come from?
- Who / what am I?
- Why do I exist?
- How should I be?
- … and what, therefore, should I do right now, in this moment?

These questions are hierarchical and chronological in nature, like a tree, with one answer birthed from the other. Reading information in this order creates a comprehensive and crystalline structure for a fully clarified frame of reality. There’s no longer a need to piece everything together, everything you need is right here.

So turn to the first page, and start your short journey to feeling clear.
