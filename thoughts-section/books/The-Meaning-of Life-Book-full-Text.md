## THE UNTOLD STORY OF ADAM AND EVE

## قدرت اراده

ONCE UPON A TIME, there was a man named Adam who lived in a perfect, kind and serene Garden. Adam spent most of his perfect life bored, wondering and feeling like something was missing. He didn’t know what it was, but he knew that there was something out there that would make him feel more at peace, at one with himself, and more excited about his life. Adam craved knowledge of the metaphysical kind. He knew that knowledge of himself, others and the Existence around him would help him figure out what he found important, and what it is he felt was truly Meaningful to do.

One day, as Adam surrendered to the lull of the same old afternoon, he fell asleep on the grass and began to dream. In his dream, Adam was visited by a mysterious voice that seemed to speak to him, its words radiating gently through his mind:

## “WHO ARE YOU, ADAM ?

You are my perfect thought,
Living in my perfect idea;
Perfection.

The one who thinks of me is you,
And the one who thinks of you is me.

You, who search for me,
Who tries to attain yourself,
Are me at that instant.

I see the realms through you.
I think through you,
I observe through you.
I evolve through you.

Do you sense what power you possess?

Pay attention to the thinker underneath the thinker;
See through the see-er,
And find me therein.

Find me neither in thought, nor in material.
When I become a thought, I am the entire skies.
When I am a substance, I am all the souls.

I am the riverbed of the river flowing tranquilly,
I am the foam of the seas flowing wildly.
Climates are me, heavens are me,
Suns are me, folding mountains are me.

## Who am I?

I am a beautiful habit,
Ugliness are my crust,
Beauties are my divine light.

If you look for me,
I AM YOU.”

The sharpness of the last word woke up Adam, as if by design. Before he could make sense of the abstract riddle, he tilted his head up to notice a book he had never seen before, lying hidden in the grass. As his curious hands picked it up, he could feel its snakeskin glaze. Adam’s eyes lit up as he turned to the first page, his mind encapsulated by this first sentence:

## “GIVE A MAN A PURPOSE, AND THE ABILITY TO ACHIEVE IT, AND HE WILL CRAWL OVER BROKEN GLASS WITH A SMILE.”

There was a beauty in this line, the way the words shone in the moonlight, reflecting back to him the light of his own mind. “Purpose,” he thought, “maybe that’s it.” Adam wanted to know if there was something about Purpose he ought to know, perhaps a perspective on life that would make him feel alive again. He knew that a Purpose could uplift the misery of one’s perfect Existence, while alleviating the suffering of an imperfect one. He saw this as nothing other than magic. Filled with curiosity and wonder, he turned to another page to be greeted by the first chapter. And it is here where our story of knowledge begins and ends, happily ever after:
“In every sacrifice we make”, he read “we reaffirm what we value, and what we don’t. Knowing what you value, or even what you live for, gives you a higher perspective. This perspective provides a sense of Purpose in terms of knowing what you should do. The ultimate question is: how can we achieve this clarity and confidence in a world that bombards us with a vast ocean of new information, values, ideas, and perspectives?

It has never been more important to discern what information is useful to us and what is not. This is where spirituality, philosophy, science, and religion come in. While religion focuses on aligning with the will of the creator and its attributes, all other sects of metaphysics have long served to make sense of our Existence and our place within it. Their goal is to satiate our seemingly innate hunger to know who we are, why we exist, how we should act, and what we therefore should do. In other words, we look to metaphysics to guide our Purpose. Whether we view ourselves as God’s children or as microscopic ants in a galactic abyss, our Frame of Reality aims to fulfil our innate need for existential clarity. And it is the fulfilment of this need that is intimately Connected with our experience of Meaning.

Whatever we believe in, we can objectively see that, as humans, we are wired to only really feel true happiness from Meaning, not pleasure. Throughout history, humans have gained this Meaning from great myths, legends and parables. After all, our minds are not really made up of atoms, but of stories we believe in. And whenever we believe in something we know is true, we hold it as Meaningful; important or valuable. Therefore, if we reverse engineered what exactly makes a story Meaningful, we can identify the most important value that resonates with us on the deepest level. If we then align with that value as a core guiding Principle, we can then direct ourselves toward a fulfilled sense of Purpose, no matter how complex the world becomes.

To find this universal value, or truth, we can employ the metacognitive thinking of Lao Tzu, summarised by his quote “Know the way broadly and see it in all things”. The ancient Chinese believed that the ultimate truth is foundational, and universal, originating from one universal source. Following this logic, to find the objective essence of Meaning within all stories is to find a foundational truth that exists across all of them. A truth that stands the test of analysis by the entire spectrum of the spiritual-to-rational thinking and, furthermore, a truth that we have known and experienced for ourselves. After all, we find stories Meaningful when we can see their patterns in the story of our own lives.”

Adam took a step back to ponder on the aspects of his own Meaningful experiences, both good and bad. Through every success or heartbreak, Adam realised that Meaningful experiences were memorable and fundamentally influenced who he had become. Looking more closely, he had found that in every Meaningful experience, he either:
I. learnt about himself or the world,
II. experienced joy/love, or
III. created something of value.
“Deep inside the roots of these patterns,” he read, “in every Meaningful experience, we see a certain direction. Meaning is experienced when a certain progress was made in the scale of either:
I. personal Growth and/or
II. Connecting deeper with something larger than ourselves.

In the very essence of Meaning, we find a dance between Growth and Connection. Here lies our golden, broad line of truth.”

Adam turned the page to follow a line from the previous side that extended in a circle on the next spread of pages. The lines split in two to form a yin and yang symbol © with “Growth” on one side and “Connection” on the other. When he looked closer at that line, he found the underlying mechanics of Meaning, written in small letters with the following words: “Here, right in between Connection and Growth, we observe life Connecting with itself, we see life growing through itself.”
“As above, so below”, he saw titled in the next chapter; “the famous words from the ancient Hermes Trismegistus as written on the ancient Emerald Tablets, further suggests that our Meaning is the Meaning of life itself. As such, our Purpose, clarity and confidence, even in our noisy world, can be gained by knowing the truth of life itself. And to know the ultimate truth about life, we would ask the most fundamental question which, also, is the Meaning of life itself. How and why? Because life is what we are at our very essence, the Meaning of our life is to reflect life’s Meaning.”

The pages taught Adam that, since he is life itself, his Meaning is life’s Meaning, and that he would find his Purpose; what he was looking for, if he dedicated himself to this truth. Put simply, Adam found that we must value the life within us, above all else. To do so, we must value learning about the essence of life itself. From a purely intellectual perspective, we should devote ourselves to learning about, loving and Connecting with the source of life within us, knowing that everytime we do so, we grow in the direction of our Meaning; Purpose, happiness and vice versa.

The book further explained Lao Tzu’s logic: “To understand the essential nature of life itself, we must even further zoom into the root of it; to understand life’s most fundamental nature, and find that all else flows from there. Even if the world becomes more complex, we can employ this wisdom to strengthen our own Frame of Reality and inspire confidence in our values and decision making. This helps us grasp the causes and impacts of things, make Connections between ideas, and cut through the noise. As such, we find ultimate fulfilment by first seeking to understand the beginning of life itself; the source of all Existence. We can view this”source” objectively, impersonally, and attempt to use our own understanding to define its nature. However, we must be aware of the limitations of doing so, as we are bound by limitations of our own mental concepts.

The following is an intellectually objective approach to conceptualise the fundamental nature of life/Existence using the shapes and symbols of human language.

In the beginning, the source of creation; life and Existence, always existed. This is because energy can neither be created nor destroyed. The source of life/creation is timeless, formless, infinite, and intelligent. Why is it infinite? Because it created the mere concept of infinity; it formed the mere fabric of that which we measure space and time. If we were to ask what the Meaning of this Existence/creation/life is, we’re actually asking, “what is the intention of an infinite and intelligent source of life/creation?” By definition, the most fundamental nature of life itself is to grow, for when life ceases to grow, it begins to die. Growth is the most fundamental nature, intention, and therefore Meaning, of life itself.

Considering the infinite and intelligent nature of the source of life/Existence, it can be deduced that life’s Growth manifests through an endless process of:
I. Self-discovery - as part of an inherently intelligent source, life seeks to deepen its understanding of its own nature and creative abilities (an infinitely intelligent force would exist in a perpetual state of learning about its infinite self)
II. Joy/Love - as a positive, nurturing force, love contributes to the overall well-being and development of life itself, aligning with this conceptual intention of an infinite/perfect source of creation.
III. Creation - our first hand experience of Existence is evidence of how life expresses its infinite creative nature.
IV. Balance - Existence itself is infinite, formless and embodies all possibilities in perfect equilibrium, representing the highest conception of balance

In summary, the Meaning of life itself is to grow, and its Growth manifests through an understanding of itself and its ability to love, expressing these through creation and perfect balance. Since Purpose is defined in the dictionary as the “intention” behind something. Our Purpose, therefore, would be to grow by Connecting with and expressing this innate intention of the life that is within us, and all around us.

We have an inner Purpose to grow through:
I. self-discovery,
II. self-love and
III. self-transcendence.

We have an outer Purpose to grow through:
I. learning about the world,
II. contributing to the world’s love, and
III. creating a better world.

Our inner and outer Purpose/goals are intertwined, since we can use our goals to learn about ourselves through our Connection to ourselves, others and the world around us. A Balance of this can be seen as the Middle Way of life; intentional, Purposeful enjoyment, of finding Meaning in the way we grow through our worldly tests. This approach is based on the aspiration to align with the will, intention or most fundamental nature, of life/Existence itself.

As we become someone who makes choices in service of our Growth-oriented Purpose, our Principles can become a reflection of the decision-making process of life itself. Principles are what we use to ensure that our decisions are aligned with our Purpose (or Growth). For the Purpose described, there are 5 core Principles that help us achieve this. [The below values are inspired by the Zoroastrian attributes of God, and therefore our spirit.] If you want to experience Meaning, then consider the following before making a decision:
I. Truth and Integrity [Asha Vahishta] - Align your choices with your clear intent and congruence, adhering to honesty, fairness, and ethical behaviour. Be genuine in your pursuits and communicate openly, ensuring that your thoughts, words, and actions are consistent with each other and with the goals of self-discovery, love, and creation.
II. Loving Devotion [Spenta Armaiti] - Emphasise compassion, empathy, and Connection in your decision-making, prioritising the well-being of yourself and others. Practice self-amusement, gratitude for simple things, and finding joy/fulfilment within yourself without needing external validation. Let love guide your choices and actions, nurturing the Growth of yourself and those around you.
III. Empowered Responsibility [Kshathra Vairya] Recognize your personal authority and power to make choices that reflect your inner and outer Purpose of Growth. Use your influence wisely to contribute to the world’s love and create a better reality for yourself and others. Embrace and focus on the process of the work itself and of interacting with the world around you.
IV. Wholeness and Balance [Haurvatat] - Adopt a Middle Way between the other Principles. Realise that intelligence, love and empowerment are all reliant on one another. Continue to grow, but learn to enjoy yourself. Sharpen your intention, but soften your desire. Place high importance on your Growth, but not enough to make you stressed. Set goals in your health, relationships and career, but try to find time to rest and relax. Seek balance as your Principle for sustainable success.
V. Presence [Vohu Manah] - Stay in Wholeness and Balance by first feeling whole and balanced within yourself. Responsibly Empower yourself by releasing energy otherwise lost in negative thoughts, and be aware of the impact you can have. As a practice of Loving Devotion, consciously aim to make your experience of the present moment more beautiful, shining that onto others. Remain in Truth & Integrity of yourself and the world by shining awareness onto your subjectivity, and seek to see the world the way it really is. Remain in remembrance of your Principles through a conscious, mindful awareness of the present moment. Have the courage to recognise and accept negative feelings in your body, and make an effort to nurture them. Your decision making abilities are vastly improved when you are free of stress.

Presence helps us live by our Purpose and Principles as it helps us let go of attachments and desires that distract us from them. Presence is becoming aware of and strengthening an innate sense of loving acceptance that comes from within. Applied correctly, Presence is an awareness and sensitivity to bodily feelings, whilst projecting a sense of equanimity onto them. More esoterically, in a flow state, and/or in a state of gratitude, you harmonise with higher natural forces, enabling vital energy to nourish you and enhance your Growth by expanding your ability to think, love and create.

Cultivating a mindful awareness of the present moment refines your understanding of yourself and the world. Over time, we become better at making peace with, or allowing, each moment to be what it is. In summary, Presence gives us the clarity of mind to make decisions in harmony with our values (Purpose/Principles) by:

- connecting us with the virtuous core of Life which we are (and Life’s/Existence’s associated intention/nature)
- detaching/healing from thoughts/emotions not in alignment with our values

## Introduction

In this final chapter, follow these steps to find your purpose in 3 days or less.

It is important to understand these three concepts.

There is a nuanced difference between “desire” and “purpose”. Desire is a trap; religion warns us of this, yet it is often used as motivational fuel to help get us where we want to be in life. However, being in a state of desire will make you miserable, you will be on a rollercoaster of good and bad days, with your mood completely dependent on the outcomes of certain efforts you make.

When you’re working towards your purpose, your worst day is better than your best day of not working on your purpose.

You’re not a slave to your mood, you’re better than that. You have the potential to remain calm and present in an ever-more chaotic world.

Remember that you can feel good in your body no matter what. Feeling good helps you tap into your intuition; your innergenius. You can enjoy the action of your work somewhat more than the fruits. You are not defined by your goals, you don’t need anything, but you do it anyway, because that’s just who you are. You do what you believe in, no matter what, and you respect yourself for it.
