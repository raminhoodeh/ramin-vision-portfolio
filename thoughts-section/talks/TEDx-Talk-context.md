**TEDx Talk**  
**Experience**  
Our own and humanity’s decision-making as a whole often neglects the bigger-picture and long-term view, a challenge evident in both individual and societal contexts, and underscored by the historical fall of advanced civilizations. 

This suggests the importance of a deeper reflection on how decisions are made. This is akin to the strategic, long-term planning seen in businesses who take stock of their values, their capabilities and their place in the market to inform their vision and therefore strategy. 

Such an approach, understanding ourselves; our values, the nature of our existence and our place within it, could guide us toward a meaningful future. 

However, this task is complex, as it involves grappling with the enigmatic nature of life and our existence on a floating rock in a vast, energetic universe.

**Values**  
Metacognition, Mindfulness, Planning, Beauty

**Vision **  
To truly recognise how beautifully amazing life really is, further than just intellectual comprehension. To truly feel and excitingly realise that you are:

* Cosmic consciousness   
* Trapped in a magical amalgamation of 40 billion years old Stardust  
* Made of/driven by energy   
* Physically one with everything/influencing everything around you

**Purpose/Mission**  
Learn about and train yourself with physiological techniques that help you embody and feel the significance of life and the beauty of existence. 

To prioritise and make the best life you want for yourself and others, based on the values, actions and meaning gained from the awe and wonder of this galactic perspective.

**Strategy**  
Learn from e.g. eastern gurus and astronauts who attained this perspective either internally or externally, who share similar values and priorities of the universal, unified, and harmonious purpose of humanity. See that, since their values and priorities are based in a psychological reality, we too can simulate this within ourselves, using mindfulness and imagination.

**Tactics**  
Set aside time to focus on your breathing and visualize yourself from a distant, cosmic perspective. Contemplate Earth's beauty, your place in it, and what truly matters to you. Recognize the power of your mind and the uniqueness of human experiences on Earth. Use this perspective to make decisions that align with your core values, returning to your daily life with a renewed and meaningful outlook.

**Benefits Recap**  
Adopting a cosmic perspective can steer politicians towards a utopian vision. Spaceflight technology's progress fosters optimism and humanity's compassion. This broad viewpoint grounds us in our place in the universe and realigns us with our core values, leading to choices that reflect what truly matters.

**TEDx Talk Conversion Story**

**Situation** :I always wondered why we, as humans, don’t live our lives according to the great mystical, magical majesty of existence. I noticed that astronauts often come back from space with a new perspective on life, but back here on Earth, we're caught up in the day-to-day and rarely stop to think about our place in the universe. I was curious about why we don't think big picture in our personal lives like we do in business. There's this gap between how businesses truthfully plan according to where and what they are, in the market, but we don’t plan according to our truthful place in existence, in the universe.

**Challenge**: And I think it’s partly because it seems difficult, somehow, to actually feel, or to recognise the beauty of our existence and to act and live from there. 

**Desire:** I then began to see the importance of using things like mindfulness to appreciate life more deeply. I wanted to help myself and people in general to see the beauty in our actual existence in this universe and feel connected to its grandeur. I believed that if we truly felt this, then we’d maybe spend less time being concerned with things that didn’t really matter, and instead make more meaningful decisions that reflected the inspiring nature of reality. 

**Decision**: That’s why I chose to talk about this topic in my TEDx talk. It was more so to share this thought, or reminder, that I thought was really needed. I challenged myself to use this talk to see if I could genuinely help people actually experience this cosmic perspective, at least for a little while. 

**Breakthrough Journey:** So I went on a journey to figure out how I could actually do that. I looked at people who already lived with this perspective and saw that, while astronauts gained this view externally, there are also eastern gurus who have gained this same perspective but through an internal process. These highly spiritual people have internally trained themselves to live in a state that more accurately reflects the miracle of our existence. Then I thought, is there a way to get the best of both worlds approach, to experience the wonderful perspective these people have, without having to go to space or meditate for decades. 

**The Epiphany:** That’s when I realised that, since we’re talking about a psychological phenomenon, we could use the power of our imagination to replicate it for ourselves, at least temporarily. I truly believe that mindfulness and visualisation exercises is the best way to feel the significance of life and the beauty of existence.

**Importance:** And so that’s why the TEDx talk involved a guided meditation which I got the audience to try… I was a bit nervous about it at first but, in the end, it went really well\! I had people telling me they felt very emotional during the experience, and that it felt more real than they’d expected. 

And partly why I feel like this is so important is because just that small decision to try this, even just once, can impact your flow of thought and decisions for basically the rest of your years to come. And in the talk, I suggested if more of us – including our leaders – could adopt this broader view, it could lead to positive changes in society. 

Overall, I think this broad viewpoint grounds us in our place in the universe and realigns us with our core values, leading to choices that reflect what truly matters.

**Call To Action:** So, if you're curious about seeing life from a wider lens, give my talk a watch and try out the exercise I shared. It's about taking small steps to align our everyday choices with the incredible universe we're a part of.
