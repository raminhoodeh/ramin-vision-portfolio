# **Framework of Metacognition**

*Written by Ramin Hoodeh. A personal and professional manifesto on thinking about the way we think. This outlines the order in which we hierarchically think about, structure and present information in chronological order: Experience [Story], Who [Values], Why [Vision], How [Strategy], What [Tactics]. Each layer or stage of thought is given an example of it in use - while the example is metaphysical in nature this is purely an intellectually-based manifesto and not a spiritual thesis. Additionally, since the “Who” Values are based on the meaning behind the experiences / stories, there is also a “hallmarks” section which explains what meaning actually is. Finally, there is an example of this way of thinking applied to the most metaphysical analysis of life itself called “Framework of Reality.”*

## **Experience** [Story]

Our experiences form the very bedrock of our belief system. These are the diverse array of external inputs, encounters, and observations that we get exposed to throughout our lives. They can be personal experiences or derived from various sources of information such as religion, science, philosophy, spirituality, personal events, cultural teachings, or even intuitive premonitions. Each experience adds a new layer to our personal knowledge, altering and shaping our understanding of the world.

**You start by describing an experience that’s either a/an… Assertion, Query, Response, Belief, Evidence**

**This source of experience could be based on…**

**Personal Reflection:** Personal experience, Intuition

**Communication/Education:** Read, Heard, Statement, Description

**Evaluation and Judgment:** Opinion, Observation, Comparison

**Societal Belief Systems:** Philosophy, Spirituality, Religion, Culture

**Empirical and Objective:** Scientific fact

**Imaginative and Creative:** Fiction story

**Intuition:** Feelings, Premonitions

**Anything which helped to…**

**Learn about oneself/the world**,

**Experienced joy/love**

**Created something internally/externally**

*E.g. Happiness arises from clarity about our Meaning. Metaphysics helps us find this because it explores our who/why/how/what about Existence i.e. our Frame of Reality. Give a man a purpose and the ability to achieve it and he’ll walk over broken glass with a smile. However, there’s too much information out there, leading to decision paralysis, we need to distill it all down.*

## **Values [Who]**

Experiences feed into the formulation of our personal values. The amalgamation of experiences we have had so far shape our perspectives and form our values - the things we find crucial and meaningful. Usually these are experiences/information we feel an emotional affinity to, or if it fits nicely with our existing frame of reality. These values fundamentally become who we are. They dictate how we perceive ourselves, how we relate to others, and how we understand our place in the world. Our values, shaped by experiences, serve as our personal compass, helping us navigate life and influencing our decisions.

**The experience would then point to something important, or valuable. Values are who we are; what we find important. And experiences form our values if the experience was meaningful.**

**Experiences (or information) are meaningful if it lead to…**

**Personal growth**

**Connecting with something larger than ourselves**

**Usually due to…**

Through struggle, transformation, reward / Overcoming challenges, Solving a problem

Reduced stress, improved relationship to present, solved a problem, sense of purpose and ability in achievement, increased feeling of gratitude to be alive.

**And therefore it was a meaningful experience/information because** we feel a connection to it, to ourselves, that points us to a transcendent capacity within the self e.g. learning about ourselves/others/the world, experiencing joy/love, creating something of value

*E.g. I value clarity because I’ve seen how a lack of clarity can lead to unhappiness (and vice versa). I value Meaning because it leads to happiness. I also know that deep down, I am Life/Existence. Therefore I should Value what Life/Existence Values if I am to embody what, in reality, I value. Since Life/Existence came from an infinite, intelligent source, its nature is infinite Growth in i) self-discovery [to infinitely learn about its infinite self], ii) joy/love [to Connect/nurture/spread itself], creation [it created everything], balance [it’s formless and embodies all possibilities in equilibrium].*

## **WHY**

The values that stem from our experiences help us uncover our “Why” - our purpose or driving force. When our values align with our actions and life’s trajectory, we feel a deep sense of purpose, energy, and gratitude for life. Our purpose may be drawn from different aspects of our experiences, like stories of overcoming adversity, appreciation of science or the universe, religious teachings, or the understanding of our unique existence. Realizing our purpose propels us to grow, to connect deeper with ourselves, others, and the world around us.

Our Values inform our “Why” i.e. what we find important to do. Our “Why” can be our Vision, which forms our Purpose, or Mission. Being clear in our line of thinking helps not only highlight but strengthen our “Why”, giving us a sense of energy/inspiration, making us feel more grateful to simply be alive .

*E.g. We realise that Meaning is the way we Grow through life’s tests, And that meaning is when we Connect with ourselves. As such, we devote ourselves to Learn, love and Connect with the source of life within us, others, and the world around us, To uncover a transcendental capacity within the ourselves. (Purpose = Intention) Being Life/Existence, our Purpose is to find Meaning in how we grow by connecting with and expressing the innate intention of Life through its tests, by balancing inner/outer Purpose (Middle Way). Our inner Purpose is self-discovery, self-love, and self-transcendence. Our outer Purpose is learning, contributing to love, and creating a better world.*

## **HOW [Strategy]**

Once we establish our “Why”, the next step is to figure out “How” to live in alignment with our values and purpose. Our “How” is the strategy we adopt to achieve our “Why”, and is therefore informed by our “Why”. It helps us focus on the task at hand, and ensure resources are deployed in service of the main goal.

*E.g. Act in service to our Purpose/Life by seeking/acting on: Truth and integrity, Loving devotion: Care for yourself and others’ well-being. Empowered responsibility: Strive for personal growth and connection. Wholeness and balance: Seek balance in truth, love, power, and enjoyment. Presence: Overcome negative emotions, relieve stress, and make clear courageous decisions.*

*Through: discovery, insight, belonging, social connection, challenge, risk-taking, awe or wonder. Therefore, we lean into experiences that cause Growth in terms of… Increased knowledge of self, others, world around us, Strengthened ability to love self, others, world around us, Increased ability to create (and/or manifest outcomes for) a better self, people and world. That causes… Deepened connection with self/others/world, Increased ability to adhere to life principles/values of truth, love, empowerment/creation.*

*Which helps us… Let go of negative attachments/distractions, Reduce stress / overcome fear, Decreased need for things outside of self for contentment, Reconcile past, Ejoy present moment, Look forward to future.*

## **WHAT [Tactics]**

Our “What” is made up of our actual day-to-day operations/activities. These are informed by our overall “How” or strategy. Your strategy would then dictate what you choose to spend time doing everyday, turning a wider plan into a daily to-do list.

*E.g. Be as you are, recognise, accept and nurture feelings within, with equanimity. Presence gives us the clarity of mind to make decisions in harmony with our values (Purpose/Principles) by: connecting us with the virtuous core of Life which we are (and Life’s/Existence’s associated intention/nature) and detaching/healing from thoughts/emotions not in alignment with our values. Allow/be grateful/Connect to the beauty of Life/consciousness in the present moment.*

*Which leads to… Increased sensitivity to the beauty that is all around us, Increased sense of purpose and belief in its attainment, Gratitude for being alive/others/world, Share this joy with others. And in the end we learn to… Transcend one’s thoughts / let go of “free will”, Be filled with the inspiration / will of our Conscience / creator / existence / life itself. Over time… Our ability to maintain this state of being, regardless of our outer circumstances, represents, much we’ve Grown as a person, and allows us to experience Meaning more often.*

# Hallmarks of an Experience that Successfully Inform One’s Values

**Accessibility**: The information or narrative must be accessible and understandable to the individual.

**Relevance**: The content must have some relevance or connection to the individual’s life, circumstances, or areas of interest to gain their attention and consideration.

**Credibility**: The source of the information or the plausibility of the narrative/experience plays a crucial role. The more credible the source or plausible the story, the more likely it is to influence one’s values.

**Consistency or Challenge:** The information or narrative either needs to align well with an individual’s existing beliefs, or provide a convincing challenge to those beliefs or values.

**Emotional Resonance**: The content should evoke an emotional response, further anchoring its significance and promoting internalization.

**Social Validation**: If the information or narrative is validated or supported by social circles, communities, or society at large, it can have a greater influence on an individual’s values.

**Resonance with Personal Goals or Desires:** If the information or narrative resonates with personal goals or desires, it can more effectively shape one’s values.

**Reflection and Internalization**: Personal reflection on the information or narrative aids in its internalization and incorporation into the individual’s value system.

**Repetition or Reinforcement**: Consistent repetition or reinforcement of the same or similar information or narratives can lead to a stronger impact on one’s values.

**Intensity or Impact**: The intensity or impact of the narrative or information, such as how much it affects the individual emotionally or mentally, can determine how much it shapes their values.

**Applicability**: Information or experiences that provide clear ways to apply the insights or lessons to the individual’s own life can be more influential.

**Evidence of Change**: The most concrete evidence that a narrative or information has influenced one’s values is a noticeable change in their thoughts, actions, or decision-making patterns.

# **Metacognitive Stratetree**

The below image is a tree diagram that represents the metacognitive approach in this document, that is more tailored to a company or product / corporate context. It’s called the “Metacognitive Stratetree” goes in the following order

- **#1 Backstory (The Soil):** Represents the experiences or stories that inspired the inception of the company.
- **#2 Values (Your Roots):** The qualities and principles derived from those stories that are deemed most important.
- **#3 Vision (Your Meaning / The Trunk):** The core identity of the company if it were to live by its values and honor its inspiration.
- **#4 Mission (Your Purpose / Thickest Branch):** The overarching goals or objectives set to achieve the vision.
- **#5 Strategy (Your Principles / Second-thickest Branch):** The specific approach or decision-making framework used to serve the mission.
- **#6 Tactics (Your Presence / Third-thickest Branch):** The day-to-day operations and internal/external gear-up to ensure decisions align with the strategy.
- **#7 Products & Services (Your Creations / Fruits and Flowers):** The final outputs that create meaningful value in relation to the overall philosophy.

This visual model directly maps to the document’s broader “Framework of Metacognition,” which moves from **Experience** up through **Tactics/Presence**.
